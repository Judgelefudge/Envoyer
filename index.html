La Mission

Ultimately, our goal is a simple one. We love rock climbing, and while we acknowledge there are a plethora of companies producing climbing holds, not too many appear to be overly concerned with what we care about most. Climbing holds give us the unique opportunity to bring the outdoor problems indoors, and with it the vast complexity and difference the rocks provide. We want that individuality accentuated, not diminished. Be creative with your climbing hold designs, and we will do our best to deliver that product to you, in whatever color, texture, or material you desire.

Le Produit

Our holds are 3D printed from sustainably sourced, and often recycled materials. Given how much we rely on nature for our hobby, we want to make sure we leave as little ecological impact as possible while producing what we love.

Crimps

Small edges, typically only large enough for the pads of your fingers. Provide poor grip and require good body control to utilize.

Jugs

Large, open holds where you can typically get most or all of your hand around. Provide excellent grip and rest potential.

Pinches

Holds of varying size and angle where the addition of your thumbs gripping power helps tremendously. Provide route flexibility as pinches can be quite easy or difficult.

Pockets

Holds with holes, most only large enough for a finger or two, with no additional gripping points. Provide almost 360° of pull direction.

Slopers

Bulbous holds with no edges or incuts for your fingers to grip, only friction and body position. Provide unique and challenging problem for the climber.

Underclings

Any hold you can get a solid grip on from the bottom to then pull your body up. Provide tricky body tension problems as well as the opportunity for dynamic moves.

Custom

Since most climbing holds are used to imitate what we might find outdoors, there is truly no limit to what shape or design a hold can take on. Try it yourself! We strive to be there every step of the way to provide you with exactly what you or your gym might be looking for to challenge its climbers.
